# Security Policy

## 🔒 Scope & Purpose

This repository (`grv-flipper-lab`) and its associated projects are built strictly for **educational, research, and engineering demonstration purposes**. The focus is on embedded systems, signal analysis, hardware security, and penetration testing methodologies.

All tools, payloads, and documentation provided in this repository are intended to be used:
1. On devices and hardware personally owned by the researcher.
2. In controlled, offline, or heavily segmented environments.
3. On systems where the researcher has explicit, authorized permission to perform security testing.

## ⚠️ Ethical Boundaries

The following activities are strictly **Out of Scope** and will not be supported, discussed, or condoned within this lab ecosystem:
- Unauthorized access or interference with physical access control systems (e.g., gates, garage doors, office badges).
- Deployment of malicious payloads on unauthorized endpoints.
- Interception, jamming, or manipulation of third-party wireless communications without consent.
- Any activity that violates local, state, or federal laws regarding radio frequency (FCC) regulations, unauthorized access (CFAA), or electronic privacy.

## 🛡️ Responsible Disclosure

If you believe you have discovered a vulnerability in the tooling, scripts, or methodologies documented within this repository, or if you identify a risk that could be exploited maliciously with the provided information, please practice responsible disclosure.

### Reporting a Vulnerability

Please do not open a public GitHub Issue for sensitive security concerns. Instead, report them privately.

- **Email**: Reach out to the repository owner directly. Ensure the subject line includes `[Security Disclosure] - Flipper Lab`.
- Please provide a detailed description of the issue, the potential impact, and any steps required to reproduce the concern.

We will make a best-effort attempt to review and respond to reports within 72 hours.

## 📜 Disclaimer

The creator of this repository assumes no liability and is not responsible for any misuse, damage, or illegal activity caused by the information, tools, or techniques contained within this lab. Users are entirely responsible for their actions and must ensure they operate within legal and ethical boundaries at all times.
